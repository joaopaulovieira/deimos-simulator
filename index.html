Deimos(Ancient Greek: Δεῖμος, pronounced [dêːmos], meaning “dread”) is the god of terror in Greek mythology.

He was a son of Ares and Aphrodite, and the twin brother of Phobos.

Deimos served to represent the feelings of dread that befell those in the midst of battle
